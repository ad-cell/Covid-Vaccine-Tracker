COVAXIN

About

  • Bharat Biotech has developed COVAXIN, India's indigenous COVID-19 vaccine, in conjunction with the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) - National Institute of Virology (NIV).
  • The two dosages are separated by four weeks. The vaccine can be stored at temperatures ranging from 2 to 8 degrees Celsius.
  • Covaxin is an inactivated vaccine, meaning it is made up of coronaviruses that have been killed, making it safe to inject into the body.

Effectiveness

  • Phase 3 results of the COVAXIN, developed by Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) in partnership with Bharat BiotechInternational Limited (BBIL),has shown an interim vaccine efficacy of 81% in preventing Covid-19.
  • The capacity of COVAXIN to neutralise the UK variant strain of SARS-CoV-2 was recently demonstrated.
  • COVAXIN has been found to induce immunity after two doses given four weeks apart in an ongoing clinical investigation.

Pricing

  • On April 29, Bharat Biotech announced a cut in the price of 'Covaxin' for states to Rs 400 per dose from the earlier Rs 600 per dose.
  • Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Jammu & Kashmir, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, and West Bengal are among the states where the corporation supplies vaccinations.

COVISHIELD

About

  • Covishield and Vaxzevria are brand names for the Oxford–AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine.
  • The vaccination has a low risk of adverse effects, such as injection site soreness, headaches, and nausea, which usually go away after a few days.
  • It is delivered by intramuscular injection and uses the modified chimpanzee adenovirus ChAdOx1 as a vector. It was developed by Oxford University and AstraZeneca.

Effectiveness

  • The COVISHIELD Vaccine has been shown in ongoing clinical trials to prevent COVID-19 disease after two doses given four to twelve weeks apart.
  • 4 weeks after receiving the second dose of COVISHIELD vaccination, you may develop a protective immune response.
  • Based on data provided by the manufacturer, the Astrazeneca-Oxford vaccine, or AZD1222, has shown to be 63% effective in an ongoing, large-scale clinical trial.

Pricing

  • According to the Times of India, the Serum Institute of India's Covishield costs between Rs 700 and Rs 900 each dose in private hospitals.
  • From May 1, the Serum Institute of India will charge Rs 400 per dosage for central and state governments and Rs 600 per dose for private clinics for its vaccine Covishield.
